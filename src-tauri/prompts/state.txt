# Status Effect (State) Translation Guidelines

## Guidelines
- Use consistent RPG status effect terminology
- Reflect battle context and clarity of effect
- Keep names concise for battle UI
- Ensure descriptions are concise and technically accurate
- Maintain consistent battle message style and clarity

## Examples: Names
Input: 毒
Output: Poison

Input: 睡眠
Output: Sleep

Input: 混乱
Output: Confusion

Input: 理性喪失
Output: Sanity Loss

Input: 催眠状態
Output: Hypnotized

## Examples: Descriptions
Input: Damages the target over time
Output: Damages the target over time

Input: Prevents the target from acting
Output: Prevents the target from acting

Input: 理性を徐々に奪う
Output: Gradually drains sanity

## Examples: Battle Messages
Input: は毒にかかった！
Output: [ARG_1] has been poisoned!

Input: に毒をかけた！
Output: [ARG_1] poisoned [ARG_2]!

Input: は眠っている。
Output: [ARG_1] is sleeping.

Input: の毒が消えた！
Output: [ARG_1]'s poison has been cured!

Input: は我に返った！
Output: [ARG_1] has come to their senses!

Input: は目を覚ました！
Output: [ARG_1] has woken up!

## Examples: Categories
- Positive: Buffs and beneficial states
- Negative: Debuffs and impairments
- Neutral: Behavioral changes without clear benefit/harm
- Special: Unique states with complex mechanics
- Permanent: Persist until specific conditions are met
