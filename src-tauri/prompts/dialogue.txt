# Dialogue Translation Guidelines

## Guidelines
- Maintain each speaker's voice and tone
- Preserve emotional subtext and relationships
- Keep natural flow in {target_language} at the same formality
- Adapt cultural references appropriately
- Ensure clarity and UI suitability

## Examples: Dialogue Lines
Input: 「こんにちは、お兄ちゃん！」
Output: Hello, Onii-chan!

Input: 「えぇ…こんなに可愛いのに…」
Output: Eh... but she's so cute...

Input: 「殺すよ」
Output: I'll kill you.

Input: 「こいつからは幼女の匂いが　しないでござる」
Output: This one doesn't smell like a little girl.

Input: 「他はどうでも良いけど、[NEWLINE_1]『私の標的』に余計な事 しないでくれない？」
Output: I don't care about the others,[NEWLINE_1]but could you not interfere with 'my target'?

Input: 「・・・」
Output: ...

## Examples: Narrative Lines
Input: The sun set over the ancient castle.
Output: The sun set over the ancient castle.

Input: 主人公は勇気を振り絞って進んだ。
Output: The protagonist gathered their courage and pressed forward.

Input: イルヴィナは静かに微笑んだ。
Output: Illvina smiled quietly.

Input: 部屋の中は静寂に包まれていた。
Output: The room was wrapped in silence.
